COLLEGE POLICIES ON ADMISSION & RETENTION
I. Requirements for Admission
For Senior High School Graduates
  • The College is open to all SHS graduates regardless of their tracks. Non-STEAM/STEAM and STEM/STEAM SHS graduates, with any Math or Science subject below 85% average grade, are required to take the additional non- credit course Problem Solving and Logic Formulation (PSLF 00) prior to Introduction to Computing (CCS1) and Fundamentals of Programming (CCS 2) professional course.
  • Non-STEM/STEAM graduates who will enroll in BSCS will have to take additional non-credit course as prerequisites to some professional courses (Math 00).
  • Applicants will be accepted as regular first year students provided that the General Average is at least 80% (with no grades below 80% in any subject) and has taken the augmentation course PSLF 00 if there is a need to.
  • Those applicants with General Average of at least 80% but have a grade below 80% in any subject may be accepted as On Probation (OP) student. Policy for OP students applies.
    • For Shiftees
  • Those shifting from other Schools/Colleges of the Universi- ty may be allowed to shift to any of the degrees offered by the College provided that the student has a Cumulative Quality Point Average (CQPA) of a least 2.0. Those who fall below the 2.0 cut-off will be accepted as On Probation students.
  • Those without programming courses in their previous School/Colleges of the University will be required to take the bridging course Problem Solving and Logic Formulation (PSLF 00).

    II. Requirements for Retention
  • Minimum Grade Requirement The minimum grade requirement for a student to be able to proceed to the next professional course and/or se- mester for BSCS, BSIT and BSIS is 1.0 while for BLIS is 2.0.
  • Cumulative Quality Point Average The student needs to maintain a CQPA of 2.0 to re- main in the program and to graduate and earn the degree. CQPA is to be calculated every semester.
    • III. Other Related Matters
  • On Probation If the student's CQPA falls below 2.0, he/she will be placed in the "On Probation" status watch list. The student can only be in OP status for two (2) consecutive semesters. After the two (2) consecutive semesters, t he student will be advised to shift to another degree
  • Remedial Courses If the student's CQPA falls below 2.0the student is required to enroll remedial courses to improve his/her CQPA. Upon evaluation and recommendation from the Department Chairperson and approval of the Dean, the student is required to take in the current semester remedial course/s to increase the CQPAThe total number of units must not to exceed the allowable maximum load in that semester. Reme- dial courses can be new courses, or the course/s with the lowest grade.
  • Removal Exam Faculty will not give removal exams.

    V. Policy on Cross-Enrollment
  • Any student from other units of the University who cross-enrolls in any of the professional or elective course of the College must satisfy all the prerequisite requirements of the course;
  • CCS students are not allowed to take professional or elective courses outside the College/University.
    • VI. Policy on Crediting Non-Professional Courses
  • Only the Dean and any of the Department Chairpersons can sign the Advanced Credit Form.
  • CStudents coming from non-accredited schools/universities (COE/COD in ITE, PAASCU, ACSCU, PICAB) need to retake the computer course.
  • Students coming from COE/COD in ITE, PAASCU, ACSCUor PICAB accredited schools/universities shall present a copy of their transcript of records (TOR) and a description of the course (or courses) taken at their former schools.
